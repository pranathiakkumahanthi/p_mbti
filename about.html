What is MBTI?

The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) helps people understand their personality, strengths, and how they interact with the world.

Understanding MBTI

MBTI is based on four key personality dimensions:
Introversion (I) / Extraversion (E) – Where you get energy
Sensing (S) / Intuition (N) – How you process information
Thinking (T) / Feeling (F) – How you make decisions
Judging (J) / Perceiving (P) – How you approach life

The 16 Personality Types

ISTJ – The Inspector

Practical, responsible, and detail-oriented. ISTJs value order, rules, and reliability.

ISFJ – The Protector

Caring, loyal, and dependable. ISFJs focus on helping others and preserving traditions.

INFJ – The Advocate

Insightful, idealistic, and creative. INFJs seek deep meaning and purpose.

INTJ – The Architect

Strategic, logical, and independent. INTJs love planning and long-term vision.

ISTP – The Virtuoso

Curious, adaptable, and hands-on problem solvers.

ISFP – The Artist

Gentle, creative, and deeply in tune with beauty and emotions.

INFP – The Mediator

Idealistic, empathetic, and driven by strong personal values.

INTP – The Thinker

Analytical, imaginative, and fascinated by ideas and theories.

ESTP – The Dynamo

Energetic, bold, and action-oriented risk takers.

ESFP – The Entertainer

Fun-loving, spontaneous, and enthusiastic people persons.

ENFP – The Campaigner

Creative, optimistic, and full of exciting ideas.

ENTP – The Debater

Quick-witted, innovative, and loves exploring possibilities.

ESTJ – The Executive

Organized, confident, and natural leaders.

ESFJ – The Consul

Warm, social, and dedicated to harmony and cooperation.

ENFJ – The Protagonist

Charismatic, inspiring, and deeply empathetic leaders.

ENTJ – The Commander

Bold, strategic, and excellent at organizing people.
